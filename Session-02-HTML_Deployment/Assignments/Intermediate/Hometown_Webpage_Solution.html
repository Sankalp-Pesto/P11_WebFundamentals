Rawatbhata, Rajasthan

Location

Rawatbhata is located near the bank of Chambal River at the foot of Aravali Mountain Range.
It is situated 50 km west of city of Kota,Rajasthan

About

Rawatbhata is a tehsil and nager Nigam in Chittorgarh District, Rajasthan, India.
The city has eight nuclear power stations, an under-construction nuclear fuel complex, and a heavy water plant.
Rawatbhata also has one of the biggest dams in Rajasthan, RanaPratap Sagar Dam, which is built on the Chambal River.
The dam is equipped with a 172 MW hydroelectric power station.

Weather

Rawatbhata has a hot semi-arid climate, characterized by hot summers, mild winters, and low precipitation.
The summer season starts from March and lasts until June, with temperatures ranging from 32°C to 44°C (90°F to 111°F).
The monsoon season starts in July and lasts until September, during which the region receives most of its rainfall.
The average annual rainfall in Rawatbhata is around 660 mm (26 inches).
Winters in Rawatbhata are mild and pleasant, with temperatures ranging from 10°C to 25°C (50°F to 77°F) between the months of October and February.
The climate is generally dry, with low humidity levels throughout the year.
Dust storms are common in the region during the summer months, particularly in May and June.

Population

As per the 2011 Census of India, the population of Rawatbhata was 23,215.

Sports

Cricket and FootBall are popular sports in Rawatbhata.
